Transhumanism: A Bubble of Promises


An economy of promises

Transhumanist Dilemma


There is an increasing trend in tech, that influences neuroscience and the research institution, in which technological promises out-growing conceptual fundamental research, begin to guide research more than concepts and understanding itself. The modern computational power, afforded by technological advances in computation, offers a new approach to research. The big-data approach. A sort of brute force approach, in which increasing amounts of computational power is thrown at research questions.

We have increased access to massive data-sets, whether it be morphological data of the brain, from diffusion tensor imaging, to high-resolution MRI and PET scan. This is afforded by technology. Yet we do not have a comprehensive understanding the brain, its emergent phenomena, or any global model of the brain and consciousness. There is a bottle neck (citation) in research, not in data, but in understanding.

While the big-data approach has produced wondrous improvements, demonstrated by advances in diffusion tensor imaging, high-resolution imaging, and these techniques have drastically changed both research and intervention. Additionally, technological development, has historically and presently enabled conceptual development. The light-microscope, enabled the confirmation of cell theory. Crispr, enables knock-out techniques to confirm gene function. The bubble of technological promises, does not refer to the advancement of technology. Nor does it really come from within the research institution. It is an external influence, but that does not make it any less influential.
